## Purpose
Capture learnings and convert them into actionable improvements that drive continuous enhancement of processes, collaboration, and outcomes.

## Why Retrospectives Matter
- **Learning Culture:** Foster psychological safety and blameless problem-solving
- **Process Improvement:** Identify friction points and optimize workflows
- **Team Cohesion:** Build trust and strengthen collaboration
- **Accountability:** Track commitments and celebrate progress
- **Organizational Memory:** Prevent repeating past mistakes

## When
After each sprint, release, or important milestone. Also after incidents.

## Structure
- What went well
- What could be improved
- Action items (owner, due date)
- Follow-up on previous action items

## Running a Retrospective

### Preparation (Before the Meeting)
- **Schedule:** Block 45-75 minutes depending on team size
- **Invite:** All team members who participated in the sprint/project
- **Materials:** Anonymous idea board (Miro, Mural, or similar)
- **Review:** Collect data (velocity, incidents, feedback) beforehand
- **Timebox:** Plan agenda with time allocations

### Meeting Structure (Recommended)
1. **Set the Stage (5 min):** Remind team of retrospective goals and prime directive
   - *Prime Directive:* "Regardless of what we discover, we understand and truly believe that everyone did the best job they could, given what they knew at the time, their skills and abilities, the resources available, and the situation at hand."

2. **Gather Data (15 min):** Team adds ideas to board
   - What went well (celebrate wins)
   - What could be improved (opportunities)
   - Action items from last retro (review status)

3. **Generate Insights (15 min):** Group similar themes and discuss patterns
   - Use dot voting to identify top priorities
   - Dig into root causes, not just symptoms

4. **Decide What to Do (15 min):** Create 2-3 actionable commitments
   - Be specific: clear owner, due date, success criteria
   - Focus on high-impact, achievable changes

5. **Close the Retrospective (5 min):** Summarize action items and appreciation
   - Shout-outs for good work and collaboration
   - Confirm next retrospective date

### Facilitation Tips
- **Psychological Safety:** Encourage honesty without fear of blame
- **Time Management:** Use a timer and keep discussions focused
- **Balanced Participation:** Ensure all voices are heard, not just the loudest
- **Anonymous Input:** Use anonymous boards if team is new or trust is building
- **Stay Positive:** Balance improvement areas with celebrating successes
- **Avoid Overload:** Prioritize 2-3 top action items to avoid overwhelming the team

## Tracking Improvements

Action items without follow-through waste retrospective time and erode trust. Establish clear tracking mechanisms.

### Action Item Lifecycle
1. **Created:** Action defined with owner, due date, success criteria
2. **In Progress:** Owner actively working on it
3. **Completed:** Success criteria met and verified
4. **Dropped:** Explicitly decided not to pursue (with rationale)

### Where to Track
- **Project Backlog:** Add as issues/tasks with "process-improvement" label
- **Dedicated Board:** Maintain a "Team Improvements" project board
- **Shared Document:** Use a living document with status tracking table

### Review Cadence
- **Weekly PM Sync:** Quick check on action item status
- **Next Retrospective:** Mandatory review of all previous action items
- **Monthly Review:** For longer-term improvements, track monthly progress

### Example Action Item Template
```markdown
## Action Item: [Title]
**Created:** [Date]
**Owner:** [Name]
**Due Date:** [Date]
**Priority:** High / Medium / Low

**Problem Statement:**
[What problem are we solving?]

**Proposed Solution:**
[What specifically will we do?]

**Success Criteria:**
[How will we know it worked?]
- [ ] [Measurable outcome 1]
- [ ] [Measurable outcome 2]

**Status:** Not Started / In Progress / Completed / Dropped
**Last Updated:** [Date]
```

### Measuring Impact
Track the effectiveness of improvements:
- **Leading Indicators:** Are we doing the new practice? (e.g., "PRs now include test coverage report")
- **Lagging Indicators:** Is the outcome improving? (e.g., "Defect rate decreased by 20%")
- **Qualitative Feedback:** Does the team feel the improvement? (e.g., "Team reports less confusion during releases")

## Continuous Improvement Culture

Retrospectives are one tool in a broader culture of learning and growth.

### Principles
- **Blameless:** Focus on systems and processes, not individuals
- **Data-Informed:** Use metrics to guide discussions, but don't ignore qualitative insights
- **Experimental:** Try improvements as experiments; it's okay if they don't work
- **Iterative:** Small, frequent changes compound over time
- **Visible:** Share learnings across teams to amplify impact
- **Celebrated:** Recognize and reward teams that improve their processes

### Beyond Retrospectives
- **Incident Post-Mortems:** Learn from production issues with blameless reviews
- **Design Reviews:** Iterate on technical and product designs before building
- **Code Reviews:** Share knowledge and improve code quality through peer feedback
- **Pair Programming:** Learn from each other while building
- **Book Clubs:** Study best practices and new techniques together
- **Show & Tell:** Demo interesting solutions or learnings to the broader team

### Measuring Continuous Improvement
Track organizational health and improvement velocity:
- **Process Metrics:** Cycle time, lead time, deployment frequency, change failure rate
- **Quality Metrics:** Defect rate, test coverage, incident frequency, MTTR
- **Team Health:** Engagement scores, retention, velocity trends, feedback sentiment
- **Action Item Completion:** What % of retrospective actions are completed?

### Improvement Ideas Repository
Maintain a backlog of potential improvements that didn't make the top 2-3:
- Review quarterly to see if priorities have shifted
- Share with other teams who might benefit
- Use in onboarding to show commitment to continuous improvement

- Did we achieve our success metrics?
- What would we do differently next time?
- What practices should we continue?

## Avoiding Retrospective Pitfalls

### Common Anti-Patterns to Avoid
- **Going Through the Motions:** Skipping retros or rushing through them
- **No Follow-Through:** Creating action items that are never completed
- **Blame Culture:** Pointing fingers instead of examining systems
- **Same Issues Every Time:** Not addressing root causes
- **Too Many Actions:** Overwhelming the team with 10+ commitments
- **Missing Key People:** Excluding important perspectives
- **No Celebration:** Only focusing on problems, not wins

### Revitalizing Stale Retrospectives
If retrospectives feel routine or unproductive:
- **Change the Format:** Try new exercises (starfish, speedboat, sailboat)
- **Rotate Facilitators:** Let different team members lead
- **Focus on Themes:** Pick one topic (e.g., "quality") for deep discussion
- **Invite Guests:** Bring in stakeholders for different perspectives
- **Review Impact:** Show data on improvements from past action items
- **Make It Fun:** Use games, icebreakers, or creative formats
